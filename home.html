About Me

Discovery

Just shy of becoming an expert, I aim to become as knowledgeable about the subject at hand as possible; exploring both business strategy and user frustrations. What are the pain points?  What isn’t working?  What is working? What should be changed?  What should not be changed?  What are the goals of this project, both short and long term?  What are its guiding principles? These are just a few of the questions I need answered before I can even begin to formulate a solution.  From end-user and stakeholder interviews, to competitive & analogous analysis, I am trying to understand the "what" and "why" of the issues and priorities driving the project. 


USER INTERVIEWS   •   COMPETITIVE + ANALOGOUS ANALYSIS

Define

Once user and business input has been collected, it is time to summarize and draw insights from the data collected.  This deliverable can be in the form of a structured report or in a simple narrative, but both need to tell a story about the user's obstacles and current behaviors. User personas, for example, help illustrate and generate a personal representation of the user, and should guide the solution throughout the design and development process. In any case, no matter what form I choose to share my findings, the findings need to be shared with the business, ensuring that my findings relate and align to the business strategy and guiding principles.

Design

The design phase is arguably the most exciting part of my process. Whether its rapid sketches on a sticky note, formal whiteboard design sessions, or detailed wireframes, seeing a solution blossom and a vision given to what the research and discovery phase has uncovered is truly invigorating. I conduct several design reviews with the client and development team early and often to validate design assumptions and raise any technical issues that will likely arise.  I find solving collectively as a team makes the final design much easier to reach.  Design, share, refine, repeat: that is my design mantra.

Test + Refine

My tool of choice for prototype building is Axure.  Axure allows me to create both low-fidelity and high-fidelity clickable prototypes.  Sharing ideas that are interactive help me to share my design intent more effective than flattened comps. A well written user test script, paired with a believable and refined prototype, maximize the tester’s experience.  And like the design phase, I always make sure the user tasks are created collaboratively and relate back to the issues discovered during the research phase.  If the tester sees and feels they are using a real product, we will receive genuine feedback. Here we have the opportunity to address feedback from user testing, address changes in business requirements, and perform QA to ensure the latest designs are free of inconsistencies for delivery to the development team.


HIGH-FIDELITY PROTOTYPE   •   USER TESTING   •   ADDRESS USER TEST FINDINGS

Deliver

If the project warrants a prototype or a style guide, this is where I refine these deliverables. When I create a style guide, I include all branding and UI elements: fonts, colors, form elements, buttons, etc...  I also write as much of the CSS for these elements so that the development team does not have to rely solely on the design comps or prototype.  If a unique design element exists, I supply detailed red-lines.  If an animation exists, I supply animation samples and timing details as well.  Providing these details ensures that the intricate nuances I create are developed as intended.  I truly believe that memorable experiences are crafted with refined details.
